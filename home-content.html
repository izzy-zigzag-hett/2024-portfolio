Millipede Animated Infographics

These animated posters were designed to loop side-by-side on a set of monitors. I can picture them in the context of a natural history museum or bug zoo.

Why millipedes?

I created this poster series to fuel my love for millipedes! I have met a few in the wild and they have become one my favorite bugs. I plan to keep one as a pet one day. My goal was to destigmatize the fear of these creatures - many view them with disdain, mixing them up with their venomous and spindley cousins.

I have a passion for zoology and biology, which prompts me to make educational work about the natural world.


Spooky Silly Monster Alphabet, 2022

Created for the Illustrative Typography RISD class. My goal with this project was to practice illustrating using Adobe Illustrator alone. I became an expert with the bezier curve tool, and feel very confident in my ability to create illustrations and icons this was in the future.

This font is based on an old-style serif font. I made the black and white version below so that it could turned into a typable font. Adobe fonts has a few illustrated fonts like this, so I thought it would be fun to try my hand at this.

Vibrations Show Poster, 2022

Commissioned by Jenna Benzinger to be the official poster of their original musical performance "Vibrations." The poster was distributed broadly across Brown campus and around Rhode Island. The image became quite iconic. Months and even years after the show, I've had strangers express delight at learning that I was behind this poster. It feels good to make something that was enjoyed so much by the community.
