The Chrysalis, 2020

Passion for learning

I created this video for a class at RISD in 4 weeks. It was inspired by a podcast my dad sent to me that explained the mystery of the chrysalis goop. I was shocked to learn about this at my grown age, and thought others would enjoy learning it too. I created the video from script to final cut, and got immense positive feedback upon sharing it with my community. This was the first animation project I ever made at this length and level of polish, and my skills have only grown from here. I hope to make more science videos in the future, as the reception indicated people enjoyed my presentation of this fun fact as much as I enjoyed making it.

Millipede Animated Infographics

These animated posters were designed to loop side-by-side on a set of monitors. I can picture them in the context of a natural history museum or bug zoo.

Why millipedes?

I created this poster series to fuel my love for millipedes! I have met a few in the wild and they have become one my favorite bugs. I plan to keep one as a pet one day. My goal was to destigmatize the fear of these creatures - many view them with disdain, mixing them up with their venomous and spindley cousins.

I have a passion for zoology and biology, which prompts me to make educational work about the natural world.

Zeta Delta Xi Rush Calendar, 2022

As the official graphic designer for my co-ed fraternity, called "Zete" for short, I was tasked with creating this calander as well as many other graphics for our events. My goal was to give an illustrative update to the previous calandars, which were clearly created in a word processor. The graphics and colorful layout were a great hit and attracted a good amount of attention to our events.

We have re-used this calendar as a template the last few years, updating the days of the week and new events using this as a base. It seems like the design will stick around for a while.

Bedtime Heroes Collectible Figurine Package Designs, 2022

The designs were made in Adobe Illustrator, including original logos and illustrations. I took inspiration from 90s and 2000s toy packaging to produce a nostalgia effect. The toys and their packages were created to be displayed in a fine-art setting rather than be produced for consumers. This enabled me to be very maximalist and hide some humor in the packaging. Despite this context, I did get an interesting introduction to package design and learned a lot about the process of recoloring and shifting assets around to fit hyper-specific printing dimensions.

Bedtime Heroes Introduction, 2023

Who are the bedtime heroes?

The Bedtime Heroes are a band of friendly critter-shaped toys that come to life at night to help various children fight off the Shadow Shufflers, which are manifestations of a child's fears. Bedtime Heroes was concieved by me in Spring of 2023 for an independent study project at RISD in which I wanted to explore the concept of a cute franschise with nefarious undertones. I created many types of media for this project, including 2D and 3D animations, 3D printed and mock-packaged toys, plushies, and even an immersive "playcave."


About the Video

This video is an attempt at creating an animated intro to the TV show itself from my made-up franchise. This process involved creating an original soundtrack using Ableton Live, as well as storyboarding and animating the project myself. I specifically wanted to challenge my typical workflow by animating the majority of it directly in After Effects, with just a few hand-drawn elements. I also enjoyed playing with textures and lighting to create a dream-like cut-paper effect.
